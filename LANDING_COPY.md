# Landing Page Copywriting Strategy

This document outlines the copywriting strategy and messaging hierarchy for the SpendScope landing page.

## Core Value Proposition
**Headline**: Stop overpaying for AI tools.
**Subheadline**: Audit your startup's AI stack in 60 seconds. Find duplicate coverage, optimize seat counts, and unlock infrastructure credits. 
**Primary CTA**: Start free audit
**Secondary CTA**: View sample report

## Problem/Solution Framework

### The Problem (Agitation)
Founders are signing up for multiple AI tools without realizing the overlapping features. You are likely paying for Copilot, Cursor, ChatGPT Plus, and Claude Pro across your engineering team, resulting in massive redundant spending.

### The Solution
SpendScope acts as your automated procurement team. We ingest your current stack, calculate redundant spending using real-world pricing data, and provide an actionable breakdown of where to cut costs without losing capabilities.

## Features Section (The "How we help")

1. **Stack Mapping**
   - *Title*: Map your entire stack
   - *Body*: See exactly what you're paying for across engineering, design, and operations.

2. **Duplicate Detection**
   - *Title*: Spot duplicate coverage
   - *Body*: Stop paying for ChatGPT Plus and Claude Pro when Cursor provides the same capabilities to your engineers.

3. **Seat Optimization**
   - *Title*: Right-size your teams
   - *Body*: Identify where you can downgrade from Enterprise to Pro tiers based on your actual team size.

## Social Proof & Trust
- "Trusted by early-stage founders to optimize runway."
- Use conservative, deterministic math. The copy must emphasize that these are *potential* savings based on public pricing, not guaranteed refunds.

## Tone of Voice
- **Authoritative but Helpful**: We are the expert procurement partner.
- **Direct**: No fluff. Startups care about runway.
- **Developer-Friendly**: Acknowledge that tools like Cursor are essential, but optimize how they are purchased.
